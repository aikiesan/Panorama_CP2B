DATA EXTRACTION CHECKLIST (What to look for in papers)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

✅ Must Have (Priority 1):
   • BMP value (L CH4/kg VS or NmL/g VS)
   • TS percentage
   • VS percentage (or VS/TS ratio)
   • Experimental conditions (T, HRT, OLR)

✅ Very Important (Priority 2):
   • C/N ratio
   • CH4 content in biogas (%)
   • pH
   • Substrate origin/source

✅ Nice to Have (Priority 3):
   • Co-digestion ratios
   • Kinetic parameters
   • Inhibition factors
   • Pre-treatment effects
   • Economic analysis

✅ References:
   • DOI (mandatory)
   • Full citation
   • Year (prefer 2015+)

TIPS FOR EFFICIENT EXTRACTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Focus on REVIEW papers first (summarize multiple studies)
2. Look for tables named: "Substrate characteristics", "BMP results", "Composition"
3. Papers from similar climates (tropical, Brazil, India) are more relevant
4. Recent papers (2020+) often cite older work - check references
5. Use PDF search (Ctrl+F): "BMP", "TS", "VS", "C/N", "composition"
6. Skip papers without clear data tables
7. Co-digestion papers often have multiple substrates = more data!
